Desrciption

Shahi Paneer is a rich and creamy North Indian curry made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a thick gravy of cream, tomatoes, onions, and a blend of aromatic spices. The term “Shahi” means “royal,” reflecting the dish's Mughlai roots and luxurious texture, often enhanced with cashew or almond paste. It is known for its mildly sweet and spicy flavor, smooth consistency, and a vibrant golden-yellow color due to turmeric and saffron.

Typically served with naan, roti, or rice, Shahi Paneer is a popular choice at Indian restaurants and festive meals. The paneer cubes absorb the rich gravy, making each bite flavorful and indulgent. The dish is often garnished with fresh cream or coriander leaves, and while it's vegetarian, it’s hearty and satisfying enough to be a main course.

Ingredients

  • Paneer – 250 grams (cubed)
  • Onion – 2 medium (roughly chopped)
  • Tomato – 2 medium (pureed or finely chopped)
  • Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tablespoon
  • Green chili – 1 (optional, slit or chopped)
  • Cashew nuts – 10-12 (soaked)
  • Fresh cream – 3 tablespoons
  • Milk – ½ cup (optional, for adjusting consistency)
  • Butter – 2 tablespoons
  • Oil – 1 tablespoon
  • Cumin seeds – 1 teaspoon
  • Garam masala – ½ teaspoon
  • Turmeric powder – ¼ teaspoon
  • Red chili powder – ½ teaspoon
  • Coriander powder – 1 teaspoon
  • Salt – to taste
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) – 1 teaspoon (crushed)
  • Saffron strands – a few (optional, soaked in warm milk)
  • Water – as needed for the gravy

Steps

  1. Soak cashews in warm water for 15–20 minutes, then blend into a smooth paste with a little water.
  2. Heat oil and butter in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chili, cook for 1–2 minutes until raw smell goes.
  5. Add tomato puree and cook till oil separates from the masala.
  6. Mix in turmeric, red chili, coriander powder, and salt, cook for a minute.
  7. Add cashew paste, stir well and cook for 2–3 minutes on low heat.
  8. Pour in cream and milk, stir to make a smooth, rich gravy; simmer for 2–3 minutes.
  9. Add paneer cubes and simmer gently for 5 minutes; add garam masala and kasuri methi.
  10. Finish with saffron milk (optional), mix gently, and turn off heat.
