Butter Chicken

Home

Description

Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a classic Indian dish of chicken cooked in a rich, creamy, and mildly spiced tomato-based gravy. It's one of the most popular Indian curries around the world, known for its vibrant orange color and buttery flavor.

The chicken is typically marinated in yogurt and spices, grilled or pan-fried, then simmered in a luscious sauce made with butter, cream, tomatoes, and aromatic spices. Best served with naan or basmati rice, it's a crowd-pleaser that balances savory, tangy, and slightly sweet flavors.

Ingredients

  • Chicken – 500 grams (boneless, cut into pieces)
  • Yogurt – ½ cup (for marinade)
  • Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tablespoon
  • Lemon juice – 1 tablespoon
  • Red chili powder – 1 teaspoon
  • Butter – 3 tablespoons
  • Oil – 1 tablespoon
  • Onion – 1 large (finely chopped)
  • Tomato puree – 1 cup
  • Fresh cream – ½ cup
  • Kasuri methi – 1 teaspoon (crushed)
  • Garam masala – ½ teaspoon
  • Salt – to taste
  • Sugar – 1 teaspoon (optional)

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt, lemon juice, red chili powder, and half of the ginger-garlic paste. Rest for 30 mins.
  2. Cook or grill the marinated chicken until slightly charred; set aside.
  3. Heat butter and oil, add chopped onions and remaining ginger-garlic paste, sauté until golden.
  4. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  5. Add cream, garam masala, kasuri methi, salt, and sugar. Mix well.
  6. Add cooked chicken and simmer for 10–15 minutes until fully absorbed.
  7. Finish with a little butter and fresh cream before serving.
